GIFTJOY

GiftJoy is a user-centered gift planning application aimed at simplifying and enhancing the gift-giving experience. Through a collaborative effort, we conceptualized and designed GiftJoy with a focus on understanding user needs and preferences. Our goal was to create an intuitive and delightful platform that makes gift planning a joyful experience for everyone involved.
  • ROLE
    UI/UX Designer Research
  • SKILLS
    Figma/Figjam Wireframing User Interview Research Prototyping
  • TIME SPAM
    3 Weeks
  • PROJECT OVERVIEW

    The Problem: Inefficient and time-consuming gift planning processes, coupled with the challenge of staying within a specified budget, contribute to user frustration and overwhelm. 

    The Solution: GIFTJOY™ simplifies planning for gift giving occasions throughout the year, reducing user’s stress by making finding the perfect gift and staying in budget easy. 

    UX Designers: Kristiina Tam (Project Manager), Claudia Diaz, and Megan Bailey

 

Interviews

Using our User Research Plan, we interviewed a total of six potential users. Based on their responses, we found that a gift planning and organizing app would be something many people would find useful. Our hypothesis was correct!

 

Affinity Mapping

We began interpreting our research by organizing the insights we gained from our interviews into an initial affinity diagram. Each color represents one of the individuals we interviewed.

 

Sorting it out

Next, we sorted those insights into categories and subcategories. This helped us to visualize where our interviewees responses overlapped, as well as revealing pain points we could resolve with our app.

 

Empathy Mapping

We examined our data further by organizing it into an empathy map, which helped us get into the head of our potential users: what do they say, think, do, and feel? The empathy map also shows pain points and potential gains our app could give users.

MEET ALEX

 
During the empathizing phase, we conducted interviews and began delving into our research, organizing it to make it easier to understand.
In the definition phase, we continued to look at our research and began to draw some conclusions. We defined some user insights and a clear problem statement.
We also did a SWOT analysis between four competitors. Gift Hero and Giftful are direct competitors and Amazon and Rocket Money are indirect competitors.

Problem Statement

User Insight



 
In the previous phase, we began to draw some more concrete conclusions based on our interview data. In the ideation phase, we continued the process of sorting the insights we gained from our interviews to decide on the features we wanted to focus on so that our app would help potential users as much as possible when they’re trying to organize their gift planning.

 

Feature Prioritization Matrix

We sorted the features we came up with into a feature prioritization matrix and voted on three features we wanted to focus on for our gift planning app: 1.Tracking gifts from year to year 2.Syncing calendar with iCalendar, Google, Outlook, or Teams 3.Budgeting features such as who, how much, and prior gift history

 

Value Proposition 

Next, we looked at how our app would benefit our users by creating a value proposition canvas. You can see how our app will meet user’s needs and relieve pain points.
The value proposition canvas shows how our features (gain creators) directly correlate with our user’s needs (gains). We also used our value proposition canvas to come up with a value proposition statement for our app.

USER FLOW

Here is a zoomed in look starting at a decision point in the “budget” portion of our user flow. Users can decide between creating a new budget, clicking on an existing budget, or going back to the home page. If they select one of the options that takes them forward, they can access all the budgeting features GiftJoy offers.
 
In the previous ideation phase, we decided on features and visualized how users would benefit from the GIFTJOY™ app. Next, in the prototyping phase, it was finally time for us to work on creating prototypes for the GIFTJOY™ app. We created a user flow, paper wireframes, digital wireframes, and a working clickable prototype. Seeing the GIFTJOY™ app in action really brought it to life!
Now that we had a clickable prototype, it was time to test it out and see what we could improve. We had four users testing the GIFTJOY™ clickable prototype, and we made iterations that improves usability to the user-friendly interface.

 

FINAL THOUGHTS

 

GIFTJOY

Going forward, we want to integrate more features into the GIFTJOY™app that resolve some of the other pain points we found in our research. 
We would like to: Add more collaboration between friends and family within the app 
Add AI recommendations based on existing lists 
Add animations letting users know when they’ve completed tasks, further adding to our user-friendly interface.
